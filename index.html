The problem

Godrej Jersey's curd packaging line was running at just 44% OEE — well below the 85% world-class benchmark. The team was consistently missing demand targets, but no one had visibility into why. 3.35 hours of production time were bleeding out every day, silently, because downtimes labelled "planned" weren't being tracked for overruns.

Key insight

The production manager assumed unplanned machine failures were the culprit. The data told a different story: planned downtimes — CIP cycles, meal breaks, hot water processes — were consistently overrunning their scheduled windows. Because they were labelled "planned," nobody flagged the overruns. That misclassification was costing ₹2.8 Cr every quarter.

Pareto analysis — top downtime causes

CIP overrun (Planned)82%
Meal break overrun (Planned)64%
Hot water process (Planned)48%
Machine setup delays (Unplanned)28%
Other unplanned stops18%

All top 3 causes were planned downtimes overrunning — not machine failures. Fixing planned overruns was the unlock.

What I built

A real-time RCA dashboard giving the production manager full visibility into exactly which downtime reason was responsible for delays — per machine, per shift. For the first time, the team could act on root cause rather than intuition.

"PatternLab's team built a non-con system with increased visibility of the factory floor, helping us with decision making on production planning and shift planning, helping us with ROI realization in a short period of time."

— Amit Sharma, Operational Excellence Head, Godrej CDPL

What I'd do differently

  • Involve floor operators earlier — they knew the real reasons CIP ran long.
  • Lock in downtime classifications on day one to avoid reclassification mid-project.
  • Quantify ₹ savings upfront to accelerate leadership buy-in.
