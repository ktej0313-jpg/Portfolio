The problem

Godrej Jersey's curd packaging line ran at just 44% OEE — well below the 85% world-class benchmark. The team was missing demand targets daily, but had zero visibility into why. Through user research and customer journey mapping, I identified delayed downtime classification as the root cause — ₹2.8 Cr/qtr was bleeding out silently because "planned" downtimes weren't being tracked for overruns.

The problem

Product analytics and user interviews revealed that fragmented popup UX was creating data gaps across the production timeline — masking ₹2.8 Cr/qtr in recoverable revenue. Operators had to navigate multiple disconnected popups to log downtime, splits, and records — causing errors, delays, and incomplete data.

What I did

  • Defined product vision and led wireframing and prototyping in Figma
  • Replaced all popups with inline forms — production entry, downtime splitting, and record merging from one uninterrupted timeline view
  • Owned GTM and change management — rollout phasing, operator training, stakeholder demos
  • Implemented dependency management and risk mitigation for shift-handover edge cases
  • Eliminated ~80% of QA-flagged errors post-launch

What I'd do differently

  • Involve floor operators earlier in the discovery phase — their input would have cut research time significantly
  • Lock in downtime classifications on day one to avoid mid-project reclassification
  • Quantify ₹ savings earlier to accelerate leadership buy-in
